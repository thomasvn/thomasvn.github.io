Work Experience

iD Tech Camps

Working at Santa Clara University iD Tech during the summer of 2015, I served as a Tech Coordinator as well as an Instructor.

Serving as a Tech Coordinator, I was often moved around to a variety of classes to help teach when my assistance was required. Often times that would mean me filling in for another instructor to teach the entire week! The courses that I taught / filled in for were all unique ranging from Java to Robotics. While I wasn't helping teach classes, I was often called to help fix tech problems that arose within the staff and the labs.

Serving as an Instructor, I taught Robotics, Scratch Programming with JavaScript, and Mobile Game Design. Each of these courses were one week long consisting of eight students. Throughout the week I introduced them to the foundations pertaining to their course and helped guide them to create a final project!

Projects

BroncoHacks

BroncoHacks was an open-ended hackathon that allowed participants' innovative minds to roam free. I worked with in a group of 5 to develop a website called roommatefindr.com which allowed users to take a survey and locate people who would be willing to share a roof within their target areas.

Hack for the Homeless

Hack for the Homeless was an event held early in 2015 that was aimed towards developing apps to help homeless people who were distributed cell phones. With a group of 4 others, our team aimed to develop an app that would allow homeless people to ping locations and descriptions and display them on a RSS feed that would tell other homeless people in the community where and when certain events were taking place -- for example clothing distribution.
